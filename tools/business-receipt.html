What this business receipt documents

A receipt records money that was received. It is different from an invoice, which normally asks a customer to pay. Use a unique receipt number and keep the corresponding invoice, contract, bank entry or payment processor record when those documents provide additional evidence.

Use the status field carefully

Select Paid in full only when the amount received equals the receipt total. Use Partial payment or Deposit received when money remains due. The generated receipt shows the remaining balance without changing the terms of the original invoice or agreement.

Tax and legal boundary

This generator does not determine whether a transaction is taxable, deductible, refundable or compliant with a local receipt rule. Add a tax ID or registration number only when it is appropriate for the seller and jurisdiction. For donations, rent, payroll or regulated sales, use the specific documentation required for that transaction.

Evidence to retain

  1. The generated receipt with a unique number.
  2. Proof of payment, such as a bank or processor record.
  3. The related invoice, order, contract or service description.
  4. Notes explaining the business purpose when relevant.

The IRS lists receipts, invoices, paid bills and payment records as supporting business documents. It also notes that multiple documents may be needed to substantiate all parts of a purchase or expense.

Business receipt questions

What should a business receipt include?

A useful receipt normally identifies the seller and customer, transaction date, receipt number, items or services, amounts, taxes, payment method and payment reference. Local invoicing and tax rules may require additional information.

Is a receipt the same as an invoice?

No. An invoice generally requests payment, while a receipt records that money was received. Keep both when they document different stages of a transaction.

Can I save the receipt as a PDF?

Yes. Generate the receipt, choose Print / PDF, and select your browser's PDF destination.

Does the tool store transaction data?

No transaction fields are sent to BillingFixPro by the receipt generator. The document is assembled in the browser. Analytics records button events without the names, descriptions or payment reference entered.

Does this receipt prove a tax deduction?

Not by itself. A receipt can be a supporting record, but tax treatment depends on the transaction, applicable law and the rest of the records supporting the income or expense.
