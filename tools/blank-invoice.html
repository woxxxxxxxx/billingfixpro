Blank invoice checklist

A blank template is most useful when it preserves the evidence another person needs to approve, pay and later reconcile the charge.

  • Unique invoice number
  • Seller and customer identities
  • Issue and payment dates
  • Specific goods or services
  • Quantity, rate and amount
  • Tax and discount breakdown
  • Total and currency
  • Payment instructions
Recordkeeping note: The IRS identifies invoices as supporting business documents and recommends organizing records so income and expenses can be traced. Keep the final invoice together with approval and proof of payment. Read the IRS guidance.

Before sending

  1. Confirm the customer name, reference and billing address.
  2. Match every charge to the agreed scope, delivery record or purchase order.
  3. Check tax treatment and identifiers for the relevant jurisdiction.
  4. Preview the PDF and verify that payment instructions are complete.
  5. Save a copy using a consistent invoice number and file name.

Frequently asked questions

What should a blank invoice include?

Include seller and customer details, a unique number, dates, itemized charges, adjustments, the total due, payment instructions and any relevant PO or project reference.

Can I save the invoice as a PDF?

Yes. Generate it, choose Print / Save PDF and select your browser's PDF destination.

Is my invoice data uploaded?

The invoice is assembled in your browser. This page does not submit invoice field contents to BillingFixPro.

Can I use a different currency?

Yes. The tool supports USD, EUR, GBP, CAD, AUD, INR and JPY.

Does this satisfy every tax rule?

No single blank invoice fits every jurisdiction. Confirm local tax identifiers, wording, rates and record-retention requirements before sending.
