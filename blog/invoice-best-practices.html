Invoice Best Practices: A 10-Point Pre-Send Review

Use a practical invoice review to prevent approval delays, payment confusion, and bookkeeping cleanup.

By BillingFixPro Editorial TeamReviewed July 24, 202612 minute read

A useful invoice does more than state an amount. It connects completed work to an approver, a due date, a payment route, and the records both sides will need later. When any one of those links is missing, an otherwise accurate invoice can sit unpaid while the client asks for clarification.

Run this review before every send. It is designed for freelancers and small teams that need a repeatable control without adding a complex billing system. The goal is not to make the document longer. The goal is to make the next action unmistakable.

Confirm the commercial identity

Use the customer legal or trading name that appears on the agreement or purchase order. Add the billing contact separately when that person is not the buyer.

Show your own business name, reply address, and a stable invoice number. A numbering pattern only works when each number is unique and the same identifier appears in the payment record.

Describe what earned the charge

Replace broad labels such as “services” with a deliverable, service period, milestone, quantity, or approved change. The description should let a reviewer match the line to evidence without reopening the entire project.

Check quantities, rates, discounts, deposits, taxes, credits, and the final arithmetic independently. If a prior payment is applied, label it as an amount already received rather than silently reducing a line item.

Make payment executable

State one due date, the accepted payment method, currency, and the information needed to complete payment. Avoid relying only on “Net 30” when a calendar date can remove doubt.

If the agreement permits a late charge, discount, or work suspension, make sure the invoice wording matches the signed terms. Do not add a new penalty at the invoice stage.

Route and retain the record

Send the invoice to the named approver and accounts-payable address, then save the sent copy with the agreement, purchase order, acceptance evidence, and later payment reference.

Record the next follow-up date when the invoice is sent. A reminder scheduled in advance is more reliable than noticing an overdue balance at month-end.

Pre-send control checklist

  1. Customer and supplier names match the underlying agreement.
  2. Invoice number is unique and the issue date is correct.
  3. Every line item can be matched to delivered work or an approved charge.
  4. Subtotal, tax, credits, prior payments, and amount due recalculate correctly.
  5. Currency, due date, and payment instructions are explicit.
  6. Purchase order or project reference is present when the client requires it.
  7. Billing and approval contacts are correct.
  8. The invoice terms do not conflict with the contract.
  9. Supporting evidence is stored with the invoice.
  10. A follow-up date is recorded before the invoice leaves the outbox.
